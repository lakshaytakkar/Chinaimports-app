# Suprans Mobile App — Brief

The Suprans App is the unified mobile destination for every client relationship across the ecosystem. Not a sub-feature of any single portal — the app IS the operating layer. Every lead, every conversation, every update, every project lives here.

---

## The strategic rethink

For weeks the focus has been on two separate portals — chinaproducts.in and chinaimports.in. Each with its own UI, its own conversion flow, its own dashboard. This is still correct for web — serious buyers on desktop expect specialized, transactional surfaces.

**But the mobile experience should not be two separate mobile websites.**

Mobile buyers don't want to remember which domain to visit for which need. They want one icon on their home screen that represents "my China sourcing and import partner." That icon is the Suprans App.

The web portals become marketing and acquisition surfaces. The app becomes the relationship surface.

---

## What the app replaces and centralizes

### Replaces WhatsApp for business communication

WhatsApp is the default Indian B2B communication channel — and that's precisely the problem. Conversations scattered across personal numbers. No centralized history when a rep leaves. No auto-routing. No AI assistance. No audit trail. No way to pin a lead to a project and watch it progress.

The app's chat system replaces WhatsApp for every ops conversation. Clients message through the app. Internal routing happens automatically. AI drafts responses. Vendors get added to relevant threads. Full history preserved. WhatsApp is still fine for quick pings, but the serious work happens in the app.

### Consolidates all Suprans verticals behind one login

Client signs in once. Based on their needs, they're routed to the right vertical:
- Dropshipping store setup? → USDrop AI
- Entity formation? → Legal Nations
- Wholesale catalog purchase? → chinaproducts.in (the web portal, loaded in-app webview)
- Custom sourcing? → chinaimports.in (web portal, loaded in-app webview)
- Franchise retail? → EazyToSell
- Travel / business trips? → GoyoTours

One app, multiple doorways. The client never has to remember which property handles what.

### Manages profile, billing, and compliance

Updated contact info, GSTIN, delivery addresses, preferred shipping modes, payment methods — all managed centrally. One invoice history across all verticals. One set of documents. One support channel.

### Pushes new updates and opportunities

When Lakshay launches something new (new SKU drop, new service offering, price changes, market insight), the app is how every existing client hears about it first. Push notifications, in-app announcements, chat-based broadcasts from the ops team.

## The four core pages

The app has exactly four main sections. That's it. Simplicity is the point.

### 1. Chat

The heart of the app. This is where every conversation between a client and the Suprans team lives.

- One-to-one chat with assigned ops manager
- Group chats for specific projects (client + ops + vendor + freight partner)
- AI-suggested quick replies for common questions
- Auto-routing of new queries to the right team member
- File sharing, voice notes, product links, document attachments
- Persistent history tied to client account (not a phone number)
- Search across all past conversations

### 2. Explore

A discovery surface showing everything Suprans offers. Clients browse verticals, see capabilities, and pick a service to engage with.

- Tile grid of all Suprans verticals (USDrop AI, Legal Nations, China Products, China Imports, EazyToSell, GoyoTours, Keeraft, La Bella Monte)
- Each tile opens a brief explainer + CTA to engage
- "I need help with..." quick-start flow that asks 3-5 questions and recommends a vertical
- New launches and updates feed

### 3. Projects

Every active engagement a client has with Suprans appears here. Think of it as the "order tracking" surface but generalized across all verticals.

- Active RFQs, orders, subscriptions, formation applications — all in one list
- Status and next-action for each
- Quick link to the chat for that specific project
- Document access per project
- Payment milestone visibility

### 4. Account

Profile, billing, settings. Standard but essential.

- Business details and contact info
- Invoice history across all verticals
- Payment methods
- Notification preferences
- Settings, privacy, help center

That's the entire app structure. Four tabs. Deep functionality within each, but the top-level simplicity is non-negotiable.

## Who uses the app

### Primary: existing Suprans clients
Someone who has already engaged with any Suprans vertical. They download the app to manage their relationship going forward. This is the main user group.

### Secondary: new leads from marketing
Instagram / LinkedIn / Google drives traffic to the Suprans website. Visitors who aren't ready to fill out a form on web get prompted to download the app and start a conversation there. The app's chat is the easiest low-friction entry point for a hesitant lead.

### Tertiary: internal ops team
The same app, with expanded admin capabilities unlocked by role, becomes the ops team's mobile workspace. They see client queues, respond to chats, assign vendors, update project statuses — all from the same app they'd hand to a client. This is important: no separate "admin app." One app, different views based on role.

---

## What the app is NOT

- **Not a marketing brochure.** The web portals handle marketing. The app is for relationships.
- **Not a catalog browser.** Catalog browsing happens on chinaproducts.in web, loaded in webview if needed. Mobile clients ordering from catalog will do so from their phone browser or tablet, not a native mobile UI that duplicates web.
- **Not a feature-rich app with 20 tabs.** Four tabs. Always four. Any new capability either fits into one of the four or doesn't ship.
- **Not a replacement for web.** Some workflows are fundamentally desktop — the ops team doing RFQ sourcing, a buyer configuring a complex private label order, accountants reconciling invoices. The app complements web; it doesn't replace it.
- **Not a chat-only app.** Chat is the core, but Projects and Account make the app a full operating layer, not just messaging.

## The core promise the app delivers

Two things, stated plainly:

### Promise 1: One place for everything Suprans

"Whatever I need from Suprans — sourcing, imports, store setup, legal entity, franchise, travel — I open one app. I talk to my team. I see my projects. I manage my account. I never have to remember which website handles what."

### Promise 2: Mobile-first but app-quality

"The app feels like Zomato or Swiggy — fast, responsive, haptic feedback, large tap targets, modern icons, smooth transitions. It doesn't feel like a web page wrapped in a mobile shell. It feels like a real native product."

Everything we build must deliver on both of these.

## Success metrics for app launch

- **3-month goal:** 1,500 active installs, 40% weekly active users, <5% uninstall rate
- **Chat engagement:** Average active client sends/receives 8+ messages per week
- **Cross-vertical:** At least 25% of app users engage with more than one Suprans vertical
- **Acquisition channel:** 30% of new leads in months 2–3 come through app downloads rather than web forms
- **Ops efficiency:** Team members handle 40% more client conversations per day than they did on WhatsApp (measured post-launch)

## Why this matters now

The Suprans ecosystem has grown to six+ verticals. Each with its own brand, domain, landing page, and ops flow. From the outside, it looks fragmented — even to existing clients. The app becomes the connective tissue that makes Suprans feel like one coherent company to the buyer, even as the back-end remains appropriately specialized.

And more practically: WhatsApp chaos is real. Every serious Indian B2B operator fights the same battle — conversations across 15 phone numbers, no searchability, no handoff between team members, no audit trail when things go wrong. The app solves this operationally for the Suprans team while giving clients a better experience than WhatsApp ever was.

This is the single highest-leverage product we can build in the next quarter.
